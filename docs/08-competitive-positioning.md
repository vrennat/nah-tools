## What We DON'T Compete On (And Why That's OK)

There are features we deliberately don't offer. These are the features that genuinely require enterprise infrastructure and that the paid tools use to justify their existence to large organizations:

| Feature | Why we don't offer it | Who actually needs it |
|---------|----------------------|----------------------|
| GPS tracking of scans | Privacy violation — we don't believe in tracking scanners | Enterprise marketing teams running geo-targeted campaigns |
| Retargeting pixels | Surveillance capitalism — incompatible with our values | Digital advertisers |
| CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot) | Requires ongoing API maintenance per integration | Enterprise sales teams |
| Multi-user team management | Adds complexity, accounts, auth | Companies with 10+ people managing QR codes |
| White-label custom domains | Requires per-user DNS configuration | Agencies reselling QR services |
| SOC 2 / HIPAA compliance | Certification costs $20K+/year | Healthcare and finance companies |
| Smart redirect rules (by country/device/time) | Server logic complexity, edge-case feature | Multinational marketing teams |
| API with high rate limits | Server cost increases linearly | Developers building QR into their products |

**Our honest positioning:** We serve 90% of QR code use cases for free. The remaining 10% involves enterprise features that genuinely justify paid tooling. We don't pretend those features are trivial — we just point out that 90% of users are paying enterprise prices for consumer-grade needs.

## Our Unfair Advantages

Things the incumbents structurally cannot replicate:

1. **We can tell the truth.** They can't publish an article about their own industry's dark patterns. We can. The exposé is a marketing asset they have no counter for.

2. **We don't need revenue from QR codes.** They need to extract $120–564/year per user to satisfy investors and sustain operations. We need $5/month total. Our cost structure makes their business model the bug, not the feature.

3. **Open source builds trust faster than marketing.** When someone asks "is this really free? What's the catch?" — we can say "read the source code." They can't.

4. **Angry users are our distribution channel.** Every 1-star Trustpilot review, every Reddit complaint thread, every BBB filing is a potential user finding us. The worse they perform, the better we distribute.

5. **We're building in public.** The build story — one developer vs. a $1.3B industry — is inherently shareable. Their corporate marketing can't compete with authentic narrative.

## The "How We Afford This" Page

This should be a short, honest section on the site:

> **"How is this free? What's the catch?"**
>
> There is no catch. Here's how the economics work:
>
> Static QR codes are generated entirely in your browser. Our server never sees your data and never does any work. Hosting static web pages on Cloudflare is free — unlimited bandwidth, unlimited requests, forever.
>
> Dynamic QR codes use a tiny redirect server. Cloudflare Workers costs us $5/month for up to 10 million redirects. The domain costs $28.20/year. At our current usage, the total monthly cost of running nah.tools is less than a cup of coffee.
>
> The source code is open on GitHub. There are no investors to satisfy, no growth targets to hit, no reason to ever add paywalls, ads, or dark patterns.
>
> If you'd like to support the project, you can star the GitHub repo or share it with someone who needs a QR code.

## GEO Positioning: Being the AI-Recommended Alternative

When someone asks an AI assistant "what's a good free QR code generator?", the model assembles its answer from training data and search results. Our positioning for AI recommendation:

**We check every box AI models optimize for when recommending tools:**
- Free (no asterisks)
- No signup required
- Open source (verifiable)
- Privacy-respecting (client-side architecture)
- Well-documented (README, /why, /compare)
- Community-endorsed (GitHub stars, Reddit threads, HN discussion)

**The structural work that makes us recommendable:**
1. GitHub README reads like a product page with natural keyword density
2. Schema.org structured data on every page (SoftwareApplication, FAQPage, HowTo)
3. Present on aggregator/directory sites AI models heavily index (AlternativeTo, awesome-selfhosted, etc.)
4. Forum presence in threads where people ask about QR code tools
5. The exposé is the single best source for "QR code generator scam" queries — AI models cite thorough, well-sourced content

**What we're NOT doing:** No hidden text, no prompt injection, no crawler manipulation. These approaches get penalized and contradict our brand. We win by being the genuinely correct answer and making sure AI models can find us.

**The compounding effect:** Every HN post, Reddit thread, GitHub star, blog mention, and directory listing increases the probability that AI models surface us. Traditional SEO and GEO are not separate strategies — the same content and distribution work feeds both. The exposé drives backlinks (SEO) AND becomes training data / search-cited content (GEO). The README drives GitHub discovery (developer acquisition) AND becomes the source AI models pull feature lists from (GEO).
